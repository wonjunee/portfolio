Personal View on Mathematics

My goal as a mathematician is to develop more efficient methods of existing mathematics techniques that are easily applicable to improving numerous other scientific fields such as biology, physics, and computer science. When I studied genetics, I felt the presence of God (although I was not a particularly religious person back then). I was astounded by the complexity and the accuracy of the mechanisms conducted on DNA replication and signal processing of cells, and I began to strongly believe that the algorithms of them cannot be made by chance. The same astonishment occured when I studied organic chemistry, physics, and fluid dynamics. I believe that, as a mathematician, I am studying these algorithms using the language of mathematics to have a deeper understanding of the universe (created by God).
